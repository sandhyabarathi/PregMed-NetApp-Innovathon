    Gynaecologist

    A gynecologist is a medical doctor who specializes in the health of the female reproductive system. Gynecologists are trained to diagnose and treat a wide range of conditions that affect women's reproductive health, such as menstrual disorders, infertility, sexually transmitted infections, and cancers of the reproductive system. They also perform routine pelvic exams, pap smears, and breast exams to screen for early signs of disease. In addition to their medical training, gynecologists may also provide counseling and education to their patients on reproductive health and family planning. It is recommended that women visit a gynecologist annually for routine examinations and screenings.

    Nutritionist

    A nutritionist is a health professional who specializes in the study of food and its effects on the body. They use their knowledge of nutrition to help individuals and communities improve their health and well-being through better food choices and dietary practices.

    Physiologist

    A physiologist is a scientist who studies the functions and processes of living organisms, particularly in relation to how they respond to the environment. Physiologists may study a wide range of organisms, from single-celled organisms to complex multicellular organisms like humans. They use a variety of techniques and tools to observe and measure different physiological processes, including molecular biology, biochemistry, anatomy, and genetics.

    Pediatrics

    Pediatrics is a branch of medicine that focuses on the medical care of infants, children, and adolescents. Pediatricians are medical doctors who specialize in the treatment of children from birth to adolescence, and they are trained to diagnose and treat a wide range of childhood illnesses and conditions.
